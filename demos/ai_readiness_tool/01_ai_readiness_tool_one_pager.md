# AI Readiness Tool

## Short Pitch

The AI Readiness Tool helps companies understand whether their data, workflows, governance, and technical architecture are ready for practical AI adoption.

It is not a legal compliance product. It is a technical and operational readiness assessment that helps teams identify risks, quick wins, and the next realistic implementation steps.

## Client Problem

Many companies want to use AI, but they do not know whether their current systems are ready.

Common issues:
- Data is scattered across documents, systems, and spreadsheets
- Ownership of data and workflows is unclear
- Security and privacy risks are not documented
- AI use cases are discussed without delivery plans
- Teams do not know whether a use case is low-risk, high-risk, or simply not worth building

## Target Clients

- SMEs exploring AI automation
- Startups adding AI features
- Consultancies helping clients with AI adoption
- Operations teams with document-heavy workflows
- Companies that need a first technical AI readiness review before investing further

## Proposed Output

The tool produces a practical readiness report with:

- AI readiness score
- Use-case classification notes
- Data quality observations
- Workflow and integration risks
- Security and privacy questions
- Governance gaps
- Quick wins
- 30-day implementation roadmap

## Demo Scenario

A fictional Belgian SME wants to use AI to process customer emails, classify requests, summarize attached documents, and suggest next actions to staff.

The demo asks:
- What data is used?
- Is personal data involved?
- Who reviews AI output?
- Where will the system run?
- What systems need integration?
- What happens if AI output is wrong?

The output is a readiness report and a practical next-step roadmap.

## Minimum Prototype

Input:
- Markdown questionnaire
- Business use-case description
- Data/system notes

Processing:
- LLM-assisted assessment using a structured prompt
- Scoring rubric
- Checklist-based risk review

Output:
- Markdown report
- Optional PDF export

## Important Notes

The EU AI Act entered into force on 1 August 2024 and applies in phases. The European Commission states that the AI Act is fully applicable from 2 August 2026, with some exceptions including prohibited practices and AI literacy from 2 February 2025, governance and GPAI obligations from 2 August 2025, and certain high-risk embedded systems from 2 August 2027.
